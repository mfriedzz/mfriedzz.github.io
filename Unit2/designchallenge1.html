How do they exhibit effective use of space, size, or balance?

Twitter uses negative or background space to display actual twitter users photos as background in its initial login page. The size of the welcome banner, and quick explaination of the product has an increased relative importance, compared to the background photo's description and date. The smaller focal point and bottom location of the site map on the login page, helps bring balance to the whole sight, contrasting with the Login page element. Google+'s default layout uses minimal white space, while keeping the scale of objects of the first page of elements you see, and then uses these same elements size in a sort of pattern below the fold.

What makes them similiar and different?

Differences: Twitter utilizes background space much more effectively then google+. Google+ uses size & balance much more randomly then twitter. THere are multiple focal points with google+, destroying the hierarchy of google+. Twitter is much more uniform in its use of balance. Similarities: They both have a sitemap on their sites, located at the top of the pages. They both have a flow from top to bottom, using size space, and color contrasts to influence hierarchy and flow.
